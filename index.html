Vivian Dorothy Maier

A woman devoted herself to American Street Photography

Vivian Maier's self-portrait

February 1, 1926 – April 21, 2009

She was a Socialist, a Feminist, a movie critic, and a tell-it-like-it-is type of person. She learned English by going to theaters, which she loved. ... She was constantly taking pictures, which she didn't show anyone.

Maloof, John on Vivian Maier - Her Discovered Work

Ttime line of Vivian Maier's life:

  • Feb. 1, 1926 - Born in New York City to a French mother and Austrian father.
  • 1930s-1950s - In early childhood, lives in New York with her mother and well-known photographer Jeanne Bertrand. Later, records show trips between the U.S. and France.
  • 1949 - Begins experimenting with photography while in France with her first camera, a Kodak Brownie box.
  • 1951 - Returns to New York area and works as a nanny.
  • 1952 - Buys a better, German-made Rolleiflex camera. During this time she travels quite a bit.
  • 1956 - Moves to Chicago's northern suburbs, working as a nanny for the Gensburg family’s three boys. There she has access to a darkroom.
  • 1970s - With the Gensburg children grown, she works for other families and loses a consistent place to develop film. Rolls of unprinted work began to collect. She switches to 35 mm color photography using Leica and other cameras. Subject matter changes from people to still life and found objects.
  • 1980s - Financial stress puts her ability to process film on hold and more undeveloped photos collect.
  • Late 1990s-Early 2000s - Puts equipment, photographs and other belongings into storage. She is homelessness for a time and also lives in a studio apartment partly paid for by the Gensburgs.
  • 2007 - The storage company auctions off her locker contents, including negatives and prints, because of failure to pay rent.
  • 2008 - Falls on ice and hits her head in downtown Chicago. Her health deteriorates.
  • April 21, 2009 - Maier Dies.
